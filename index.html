LAXMAN'S STORY

“I had to drop out of college because I was needed at home. My mother was not well - she is still not - and there was no money for her treatment, let alone my education. I simply could not have stayed in Bhubaneshwar and pursued my education in arts, knowing what was going on at home; thus, I came back and started helping my father in making some money to run the house.”

20-year-old Laxman Bhuyan is well-known as a mural artist in his community of Saura Adivasis in the Gajapati district of Odisha. He has done murals in Bhubaneshwar, Cuttack, Kalahandi and Gajapati - some for the government and some for private organisations. He got paid for some, and for others, he did not. He doesn’t mind, though, as each project has been a learning experience.

The desire for more learning experiences is why he decided to start his education again. “I am planning to go to Sir J J School of Arts in Mumbai, but if that doesn’t work, I will go to Kolkata.” When asked how he will fund his studies, he sheepishly shrugs and says he has no idea but will figure something out. Regardless of how he gets there, Laxman is confident that he must refine his skills to carry on his work. As he shows off some of his pieces - imitations of Vincent van Gogh’s “Starry Night” and “Sunflowers” - he explains further:

“I am an Adivasi, and no one thinks an artist, say someone like Van Gogh, could exist in such a small, remote village like this. Even the people from my own community do not think art is something that can be pursued by us, since we have always traditionally been farmers and laborers. I think differently. I know the same talent exists here that exists in a place like Delhi. It’s just that the path for us is so much more difficult. I know so many young artists here, who probably will not pursue their passion because of societal demands and expectations, and that’s the tragedy here.”

“I have already bought an easel recently, but a lot more needs to be done.”
